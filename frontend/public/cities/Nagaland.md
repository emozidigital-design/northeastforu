# Kohima

## Kohima War Cemetery

The Kohima War Cemetery commemorates soldiers who lost their lives during the Battle of Kohima in World War II, considered one of the most important battles fought in the region.

### History

The cemetery was established by the Commonwealth War Graves Commission to honor Allied soldiers who defended Kohima against Japanese forces in 1944.

### Why Visit

* Important World War II historical site
* Peaceful memorial atmosphere
* Beautifully maintained landscape
* Educational heritage tourism experience

## Kisama Heritage Village

Kisama Heritage Village is the main venue of the Hornbill Festival and showcases traditional Naga tribal architecture and culture.

### History

The village was developed to preserve and promote the cultural heritage of Nagaland’s tribal communities.

### Why Visit

* Experience traditional Naga culture
* Venue of the Hornbill Festival
* Traditional tribal houses and performances
* Cultural photography opportunities

---

## Japfu Peak

Japfu Peak is one of Nagaland’s highest mountains and a popular trekking destination.

### History

The mountain has long been part of local tribal territories and traditional trekking routes.

### Why Visit

* Scenic trekking trails
* Panoramic mountain landscapes
* Rhododendron forests
* Adventure tourism experience

# Dimapur

## Kachari Ruins

The Kachari Ruins are ancient mushroom-shaped stone pillars connected to the Dimasa Kingdom.

### History

These ruins are believed to date back several centuries and represent remnants of the old Kachari civilization that once ruled parts of the region.

### Why Visit

* Archaeological and historical significance
* Unique stone architecture
* Educational tourism destination
* Insight into ancient regional history

---

## Hong Kong Market

One of the busiest commercial areas in Nagaland known for imported goods and local trade.

### History

The market developed as a major trading hub due to Dimapur’s strategic location and commercial growth.

### Why Visit

* Shopping and local market exploration
* Experience urban Nagaland culture
* Food and handicraft stalls
* Vibrant local atmosphere

---

## Triple Falls

Triple Falls is a scenic waterfall destination located near Dimapur.

### History

The waterfall area has traditionally been a local recreational spot surrounded by forest landscapes.

### Why Visit

* Scenic natural beauty
* Photography opportunities
* Nature tourism experience
* Relaxing short excursion

# Dzukou Valley

## Dzukou Valley Trek

Dzukou Valley is among the most famous trekking destinations in North East India.

### History

The valley has long been associated with local tribal communities and seasonal grazing routes before becoming a popular trekking destination.

### Why Visit

* Rolling green mountain landscapes
* Seasonal wildflower blooms
* Camping and trekking opportunities
* One of Northeast India’s most scenic valleys

---

## Seasonal Flower Valleys

During monsoon and summer, the valley becomes covered with rare flowers and alpine vegetation.

### History

The unique climate and altitude created a biodiversity-rich ecosystem across the valley.

### Why Visit

* Valley of flowers experience
* Nature and landscape photography
* Rare Himalayan flora
* Peaceful mountain environment

# Hornbill Festival

## Cultural Performances

The festival showcases dances, music, games, and traditions of Nagaland’s tribes.

### History

The Hornbill Festival was launched by the Government of Nagaland to preserve and promote tribal heritage and tourism.

### Why Visit

* Witness authentic tribal traditions
* Traditional music and dance
* Local food and handicrafts
* Largest cultural festival in Northeast India

---

## Traditional Morungs

Morungs are traditional tribal youth dormitories reconstructed during the festival.

### History

Historically, morungs served as centers for tribal education, training, and community life.

### Why Visit

* Traditional Naga architecture
* Learn about tribal history
* Cultural immersion experience
* Heritage photography opportunities

# Mon District

## Longwa Village

Longwa Village is famous because the international border between India and Myanmar passes through the village.

### History

The village is inhabited by the Konyak tribe, historically known for its warrior traditions and unique cultural practices.

### Why Visit

* Unique international border village
* Experience Konyak tribal culture
* Traditional village architecture
* Cultural and anthropological tourism

---

## Konyak Tribal Settlements

The villages preserve traditional customs, ornaments, wood carving, and local craftsmanship.

### History

The Konyaks were historically among the most powerful tribal communities in the region.

### Why Visit

* Authentic tribal heritage
* Traditional tattoos and ornaments
* Cultural interaction opportunities
* Rare heritage tourism experience

# Phek & Tuensang

## Shilloi Lake

Shilloi Lake is a scenic lake located in Phek district surrounded by hills and forests.

### History

Local folklore associates the lake with spiritual beliefs and traditional tribal stories.

### Why Visit

* Scenic lake views
* Peaceful natural environment
* Photography and relaxation
* Offbeat tourism destination

---

## Tribal Villages

The districts contain several traditional villages preserving local customs and lifestyles.

### History

These villages have remained culturally important centers for tribal traditions and agricultural practices.

### Why Visit

* Experience authentic rural Nagaland
* Traditional food and crafts
* Cultural tourism opportunities
* Scenic mountain landscapes

---

## Mountain Landscapes and Trails

The hilly terrain of Phek and Tuensang offers scenic trekking and road trip experiences.

### History

The mountain routes historically connected tribal communities across eastern Nagaland.

### Why Visit

* Scenic road journeys
* Trekking and hiking
* Remote nature tourism
* Untouched landscapes
