# Shillong

## Shillong Peak

Shillong Peak is the highest viewpoint in the city and offers panoramic views of Shillong, surrounding hills, and distant valleys. The area has historical importance as a strategic observation point during the colonial era.

### Why Visit

* Best panoramic view of Shillong
* Excellent sunrise and sunset photography
* Cool climate and scenic surroundings
* Popular viewpoint for tourists and nature lovers

---

## Ward’s Lake

Ward’s Lake is an artificial lake built during the British colonial period and remains one of Shillong’s most visited urban attractions.

### Why Visit

* Peaceful boating experience
* Landscaped gardens and walking paths
* Family-friendly atmosphere
* Central location within the city

---

## Elephant Falls

Elephant Falls is one of Meghalaya’s most famous waterfalls, named during the British period after an elephant-shaped rock once found nearby.

### Why Visit

* Multi-tiered scenic waterfall
* Easy accessibility from Shillong
* Ideal photography location
* Surrounded by lush greenery

---

## Police Bazaar

Police Bazaar is Shillong’s primary commercial and shopping district known for local markets, cafés, and street food culture.

### Why Visit

* Shopping for local handicrafts and souvenirs
* Experience Shillong’s urban culture
* Restaurants and café exploration
* Vibrant evening atmosphere

---

## Sunrise and Sunset Points

# Cherrapunji

## Nohkalikai Falls

Nohkalikai Falls is the tallest plunge waterfall in India and is associated with local Khasi folklore and legends.

### Why Visit

* One of India’s highest waterfalls
* Spectacular valley viewpoints
* Major photography attraction
* Best during monsoon season

---

## Double Decker Living Root Bridge

These living root bridges are naturally engineered by Khasi communities using the roots of rubber trees over several generations.

### Why Visit

* Unique bio-engineering heritage
* Scenic trekking experience
* UNESCO tentative heritage significance
* Dense rainforest landscapes

---

## Mawsmai Cave

Mawsmai Cave is one of Meghalaya’s most accessible limestone cave systems.

### Why Visit

* Explore natural cave formations
* Adventure tourism experience
* Geological significance
* Family-friendly cave exploration

---

## Seven Sisters Falls

This seasonal waterfall consists of multiple parallel streams flowing down limestone cliffs.

### Why Visit

* Panoramic waterfall views
* Excellent during monsoon season
* Scenic photography destination
* Dramatic cliff landscapes

---

# Mawsynram

## Mawsynram Village

Mawsynram is globally recognized as one of the wettest inhabited places on Earth.

### Why Visit

* Experience extreme monsoon landscapes
* Unique climate and weather conditions
* Scenic cloud-covered valleys
* Rural Khasi village culture

---

## Mawjymbuin Cave

This cave is known for naturally formed limestone stalagmites that hold religious significance.

### Why Visit

* Natural cave formations
* Religious and geological importance
* Adventure tourism attraction
* Scenic surroundings

---

## Monsoon Waterfalls

The heavy rainfall creates numerous seasonal waterfalls across the region.

### Why Visit

* Spectacular monsoon scenery
* Ideal for photography

* Refreshing mountain environment

# Dawki

## Umngot River

The Umngot River is famous for its crystal-clear waters where boats appear to float on transparent glass.

### Why Visit

* One of India’s clearest rivers
* Boating and kayaking experiences
* Exceptional photography destination
* Scenic mountain and river landscapes

---

## Shnongpdeng

Shnongpdeng is an adventure tourism village located near Dawki.

### Why Visit

* Camping and riverside stays
* Adventure sports and ziplining
* Cliff jumping and kayaking
* Popular road trip destination

---

## Dawki Suspension Bridge

Built during the British period, the bridge remains one of the historic landmarks of the region.

### Why Visit

* Historical significance
* Scenic river views

* Cultural and transportation heritage

# Umiam

## Umiam Lake

Umiam Lake is a large man-made reservoir created during the construction of a hydroelectric project in Meghalaya.

### Why Visit

* Scenic lake surrounded by hills
* Boating and water sports
* Excellent sunrise and sunset views
* Relaxing stop between Guwahati and Shillong

---

## Water Sports Complex

The lake area includes recreational facilities for adventure and leisure tourism.

### Why Visit

* Kayaking and boating activities
* Family-friendly tourism
* Adventure tourism opportunities
* Lakeside recreation

---

## Viewpoints Around Umiam

Several viewpoints overlook the lake and surrounding valleys.

### Why Visit

* Panoramic landscape photography
