# Agartala

## Ujjayanta Palace

Ujjayanta Palace is the most iconic royal landmark in Tripura and now functions as a state museum.

### History

Built in the early 20th century by Maharaja Radha Kishore Manikya, the palace served as the royal residence of the Tripura kings.

### Why Visit

* Grand Indo-Saracenic architecture
* Museum showcasing tribal and royal heritage
* Historical significance of the Tripura kingdom
* Beautiful gardens and palace surroundings

---

## Jagannath Temple

This temple is one of Agartala’s major religious landmarks dedicated to Lord Jagannath.

### History

Constructed by the Tripura royal family, the temple reflects Hindu architectural influences blended with local design styles.

### Why Visit

* Spiritual and cultural importance
* Scenic hilltop setting
* Traditional temple architecture
* Peaceful atmosphere

---

## Heritage Park

Heritage Park displays miniature replicas of Tripura’s major attractions and cultural monuments.

### History

The park was developed to promote awareness about the state’s tourism and heritage sites.

### Why Visit

* Educational tourism experience
* Landscaped gardens and recreation
* Overview of Tripura’s attractions
* Family-friendly destination

# Neermahal

## Neermahal Palace

Neermahal is the only water palace in Eastern India located in the middle of Rudrasagar Lake.

### History

The palace was built by Maharaja Bir Bikram Kishore Manikya during the 1930s as a royal summer retreat.

### Why Visit

* Unique lake palace architecture
* Boat rides across Rudrasagar Lake
* Royal heritage tourism
* Excellent sunset photography

---

## Rudrasagar Lake

The lake surrounding Neermahal is ecologically important and supports birdlife and wetland ecosystems.

### History

The lake has historically supported fishing communities and royal recreation activities.

### Why Visit

* Scenic boating experience
* Birdwatching opportunities
* Peaceful natural surroundings
* Lakeside tourism experience

# Unakoti

## Rock-Cut Sculptures

Unakoti is famous for massive rock carvings of Hindu deities spread across forested hills.

### History

The sculptures are believed to date back several centuries and are associated with Shaivite traditions and local legends.

### Why Visit

* One of Northeast India’s most important archaeological sites
* Giant stone carvings and sculptures
* Religious and historical significance
* Unique heritage photography destination

---

## Unakoti Waterfalls and Forest Trails

The surrounding hills contain streams, waterfalls, and walking trails.

### History

The natural landscape around Unakoti evolved as an important spiritual and pilgrimage environment.

### Why Visit

* Nature and heritage combined
* Scenic trekking routes
* Monsoon tourism attraction
* Peaceful forest landscapes

### Why Visit

# Sepahijala

## Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary

Sepahijala is one of Tripura’s most important wildlife conservation areas.

### History

The sanctuary was established to protect regional biodiversity and promote eco-tourism.

### Why Visit

* Wildlife and birdwatching
* Forest and lake ecosystems
* Eco-tourism destination
* Family-friendly nature tourism

---

## Botanical and Zoological Areas

The sanctuary includes botanical gardens and zoological sections for conservation and education.

### History

These areas were developed to preserve plant species and support wildlife awareness programs.

### Why Visit

* Educational tourism experience
* Nature and environmental learning
* Photography opportunities
* Recreation within forest surroundings

# Pilak & Boxanagar

## Pilak Archaeological Site

Pilak contains ancient Buddhist and Hindu archaeological remains spread across southern Tripura.

### History

The site dates back to early medieval periods and reflects cultural exchanges between Indian and Southeast Asian civilizations.

### Why Visit

* Ancient sculptures and ruins
* Archaeological importance
* Heritage tourism experience
* Historical insight into Tripura’s past

---

## Boxanagar Historical Ruins

Boxanagar contains ancient structural remains and archaeological discoveries connected to Buddhist settlements.

### History

Excavations revealed remnants of monasteries and historical structures indicating the spread of Buddhism in the region.

### Why Visit

* Lesser-known historical destination
* Archaeological exploration
* Cultural heritage significance
* Offbeat tourism experience
